# Emergent Arrow of Time: A Bird's-Eye Survey

Status markers: **[W]** = widely accepted; **[S]** = speculative or contested.

The problem has two levels. The *kinematic* problem asks why time-symmetric laws
appear to pick out a direction. The *dynamic* problem asks how that direction
propagates through entropy, records, geometry, and quantum state. Most approaches
address one level; several mechanisms likely cooperate.

---

## 1. Thermodynamic / Statistical-Mechanics Approaches

### 1.1 Boltzmann and the Statistical Second Law **[W]**
The second law is statistical: most microstates compatible with low-entropy initial
conditions evolve toward higher entropy. Loschmidt's reversibility objection and
Zermelo's recurrence objection are answered by pointing to the extreme atypicality
of the initial state.

*Limitation*: Does not explain *why* the initial state was low-entropy. The
Boltzmann-brain problem makes the "fluctuation from high-entropy background" picture
empirically untenable.

*Key refs*: Boltzmann (1896–98); Loschmidt, Wien. Ber. 73 (1876).

### 1.2 Past Hypothesis (Albert, Loewer) **[W/S]**
Postulate that the universe began in an exceptionally low-entropy macrostate.
Combined with time-symmetric microscopic laws and a uniform probability measure,
this generates all arrows: thermodynamic, radiation, psychological, cosmological.

*Limitation*: Stipulated, not derived. Recent quantum reformulation: the initial
state had low *entanglement entropy* (Goldstein, Lebowitz, Tumulka, arXiv:2405.03418,
2024).

*Relation to this manuscript*: The confinement phase of the Z₂ gauge field is a
candidate microscopic realization of the Past Hypothesis — the confined spin-network
state is the low-entanglement initial condition.

### 1.3 Fluctuation Theorems (Jarzynski, Crooks) **[W]**
Jarzynski equality: ⟨e^{-βW}⟩ = e^{-βΔF}. Crooks theorem: ratio of forward to
reverse trajectory probabilities is proportional to dissipation. Second law emerges
as an average over fluctuations. Experimentally verified in colloids, RNA folding.

*Limitation*: Consistency results, not an explanation at the cosmological level.
Require an equilibrium reference state inapplicable to cosmology.

*Key refs*: Jarzynski, PRL 78 (1997); Crooks, PRE 60 (1999).

### 1.4 England: Dissipation-Driven Adaptation **[S]**
Under time-asymmetric driving, random assemblies are statistically drawn toward
configurations that maximize entropy production. Self-replicating structures are
thermodynamically favored because they dissipate more efficiently.

*Key refs*: England, J. Chem. Phys. 139 (2013).

## 2. Quantum-Mechanical Approaches

### 2.1 Maccone (2009) and Jennings-Rudolph Reply **[S]**
Entropy-decreasing processes necessarily erase correlations with the observer,
leaving no information trail. Only entropy-increasing processes leave records.
The arrow is *selection*: we can only remember events that increased entropy.

*Reply* (Jennings-Rudolph, PRL 104, 148901, 2010): The argument uses classical
mutual information; the quantum version does not close the argument. Maccone
acknowledged partial validity.

*Key refs*: Maccone, PRL 103, 080401 (2009), arXiv:0802.0438;
Jennings-Rudolph, arXiv:0909.1726.

### 2.2 Linden-Popescu-Short-Winter: Quantum Thermalization **[W]**
For almost all pure states of a large composite system, any small subsystem is close
to its reduced thermal state for almost all times. Thermalization is the *generic*
behavior of quantum mechanics, not exceptional.

*Limitation*: Time-symmetric result — says subsystem is near equilibrium for most
times, not that it *approaches* equilibrium directionally. Still requires Past
Hypothesis as input for the asymmetric approach.

*Key refs*: Linden et al., PRE 79, 061103 (2009), arXiv:0812.2385.

### 2.3 Page-Wootters Mechanism / Relational Time **[W]**
In a globally constrained quantum universe (Wheeler-DeWitt: H|Ψ⟩ = 0), physical
time emerges from quantum correlations: conditioning on a "clock" subsystem, the
rest evolves unitarily relative to it.

*Limitation*: Direction-neutral. Generates relational time, not a directed arrow.

*Key refs*: Page & Wootters, PRD 27, 2885 (1983).

### 2.4 Höhn-Smith-Lock: Trinity of Relational Quantum Dynamics **[W]**
Establishes rigorous equivalence between Dirac quantization with relational
observables, Page-Wootters formalism, and symmetry-reduced Heisenberg picture.
The most technically complete treatment of relational time in QG.

*Limitation*: Still direction-neutral.

*Relation to this manuscript*: Trinity provides the formal scaffolding. The
confinement approach adds directional structure that Trinity lacks.

*Key refs*: Höhn, Smith, Lock, PRD 104, 066001 (2021), arXiv:1912.00033.

## 3. Cosmological / Gravitational Approaches

### 3.1 Barbour-Koslowski-Mercati: Janus Point **[S]**
In the Newtonian N-body problem with zero total energy and angular momentum,
complexity (variance of mutual separations in shape space) grows irreversibly in
both directions from a unique minimum-complexity point. No special initial
conditions required; the arrow is generic in shape space.

*Limitation*: Established for classical Newtonian gravity; extension to GR and QG
incomplete. Two-sided arrow requires observers on both sides to be equally real.

*Key refs*: Barbour, Koslowski, Mercati, arXiv:1310.5167; PRL 113, 181101 (2014).

### 3.2 Penrose: Weyl Curvature Hypothesis **[W/S]**
The Big Bang had vanishing Weyl curvature (zero gravitational entropy). As
structure forms, Weyl curvature grows. The arrow is the gradient from the
Weyl-flat initial state to the Weyl-curved final state (black holes).

*Descriptively* accepted; not derived from a dynamical mechanism.

*Relation to this manuscript*: Vanishing initial Weyl curvature ↔ homogeneous
confined spin-network state. Confinement could explain *why* Weyl curvature
starts small.

### 3.3 Bojowald: Loop Quantum Cosmology **[W/S]**
Quantum geometry provides a repulsive force at Planck density, replacing the Big
Bang singularity with a quantum bounce. The pre-bounce branch has a time-reversed
arrow; the post-bounce branch has ours.

*Limitation*: Arrow is still imported from initial conditions on the quantum state;
not generated dynamically. Bounce does not automatically produce an arrow
(arXiv:2405.01380, 2024).

*Key refs*: Bojowald, Living Rev. Relativ. 8:11 (2005).

### 3.4 Sakharov Conditions / CP Violation **[W]**
Matter-antimatter asymmetry requires baryon number violation, C/CP violation, and
departure from thermal equilibrium. CP violation implies T violation (CPT), giving
a microscopic time asymmetry.

*Relation to this manuscript*: Z₂ confinement selects time orientation at the level
of spacetime geometry; Sakharov mechanism selects matter over antimatter at the level
of particle content. Conceptually analogous but operating at different levels.

## 4. Quantum-Gravity Approaches

### 4.1 Carroll-Chen: Spontaneous Inflation / Two-Sided Arrow **[S]**
Starting from a de Sitter vacuum, inflation nucleates in both temporal directions.
A symmetric spacetime arises with both halves having arrows pointing away from a
minimum-entropy Cauchy surface. Low-entropy state arises from de Sitter metastability,
not a stipulated boundary condition.

*Limitation*: Depends on string landscape and de Sitter stability. Measure problem
in eternal inflation unsolved.

*Key refs*: Carroll & Chen, arXiv:hep-th/0410270 (2004).

### 4.2 Connes-Rovelli: Thermal Time Hypothesis **[S]**
In a generally covariant theory with no preferred time, *thermal time* is defined
via the Tomita-Takesaki modular flow of the physical state on the observable algebra.
Time is the flow generated by the KMS state.

*Limitation*: Time is state-relative; the "right" state must be selected from
outside the framework. Arrow requires the KMS state to have built-in directionality.

*Relation to this manuscript*: The deconfined Z₂ phase defines the KMS state that
Connes-Rovelli use as input — confinement provides a concrete origin for thermal time.

*Key refs*: Connes & Rovelli, CQG 11 (1994), arXiv:gr-qc/9406019.

### 4.3 Chirco-Haggard-Riello-Rovelli: Covariant Statistical Mechanics in LQG **[S]**
Einstein's equations can be derived from the quantum gravitational Hadamard condition
(local thermal equilibrium of the gravitational field) without hidden degrees of
freedom. Thermal time in LQG emerges from the spin-foam quantum state.

*Limitation*: Arrow still input via KMS state assumption.

*Relation to this manuscript*: Closest existing LQG work to this paper. Confinement
transition provides the *origin* of the thermal state used as input here.

*Key refs*: Chirco, Haggard, Riello, Rovelli, PRD 90, 044044 (2014);
Chirco, Haggard, Rovelli, PRD 88, 084027 (2013), arXiv:1309.0777.

### 4.4 Christodoulou-Riello-Rovelli: Fermions and Tetrad Orientation **[S]**
Fermions couple to the tetrad, not just the metric. Spacetimes with same metric but
tetrads of opposite orientation are physically distinguishable by fermion interference.
A negative-lapse region represents a time-reversed spacetime.

*Relation to this manuscript*: **Direct precursor.** The "two intertwiner states as
two time orientations" generalizes this from a single tetrad to the full spin-network
state. The confinement transition corresponds to global coherent selection of one
tetrad orientation across the network.

*Key refs*: arXiv:1206.3903.

### 4.5 Rovelli: Relational Time and Partial Observables **[W]**
Physical observables are correlations between partial observables. Time is a partial
observable; physical predictions concern correlations with a clock.

*Limitation*: Direction-neutral.

*Key refs*: Rovelli, PRD 65, 124013 (2002), arXiv:gr-qc/0110035.

## 5. Information-Theoretic Approaches

### 5.1 Zurek: Decoherence, Einselection, Quantum Darwinism **[W]**
Environmentally induced superselection (einselection) selects pointer states stable
under interaction with the environment. The environment broadcasts copies redundantly
(Quantum Darwinism). Classicality emerges from information-theoretic filtering;
the arrow appears because this process is irreversible.

*Limitation*: Irreversibility is relative to coarse-graining; still requires
low-entropy initial conditions.

*Relation to this manuscript*: Decoherence is the *output* of the confinement
mechanism — once the Z₂ field deconfines and a time direction is established,
decoherence propagates it into the classical world.

### 5.2 Marletto-Vedral: Constructor Theory of Time **[S]**
Time is characterized by the existence of cyclic physical devices (temporal
constructors) that can repeatedly implement specific transformations. Arrow emerges
from the fact that entropy-increasing tasks are possible while their reversal is not,
expressed as a counterfactual constraint (May 2025, arXiv:2505.08692).

*Very new*; quantitative predictions require connection to a specific physical theory.

## 6. Situating the Manuscript's Approach

The manuscript proposes: **arrow of time ← confinement-deconfinement transition of
Z₂ gauge field on spin networks in LQG.**

Distinctive features:
- Not a boundary condition (vs. Past Hypothesis, Penrose)
- Not semi-classical (vs. Carroll-Chen, Barbour-Koslowski-Mercati)
- Not state-relative (vs. thermal time, which requires a KMS state as input)
- Direction-generating, not merely direction-allowing (vs. Page-Wootters, Trinity)
- Gauge-invariant order parameter (Wilson loop, not local condensate) — Elitzur-safe
- Fully background-independent and quantum-gravitational

The main open challenge: show quantitatively that the Wilson loop on the spin network
transitions from area law (confined, no arrow) to perimeter law (deconfined, arrow)
at a specific coupling, and identify this transition with the cosmological epoch where
the thermodynamic arrow becomes established.
