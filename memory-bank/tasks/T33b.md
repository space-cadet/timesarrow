## Objective

Run Polyakov loop scans on the diamond lattice to verify the Z₂ gauge transition persists on a 4-valent 3D complex. This validates robustness to graph geometry.

## Method

### Revised Polyakov Loop Definition

- **Designate a Euclidean-time foliation** of the periodic complex: a preferred direction τ and its orthogonal spatial slice
- Define Polyakov loop as the product of σ^z_e along the non-contractible cycle in the τ direction
- Alternatively: measure **named winding Wilson loops** along each of the three independent H₁ generators

### Execution Order

1. **Cheap coarse pilot:** L=6, 5k thermal + 5k measure, β = 0.4–1.0 in steps of 0.05. Find approximate β_c.
2. **Fine scan:** L=8, 10, 12 around the pilot β_c, 50k + 50k sweeps.

## Expected Output

- `numerics/data/t33-diamond-L{8,10,12}-{date}.json`
- χ_P, ⟨P̄⟩, U_P as functions of β
- Compare to cubic-lattice results: same universality class, different β_c

## Notes

**Key correction from review:** Do not preselect β ≈ 0.65–0.75. The action normalization and plaquette structure differ from cubic, so β_c is not known a priori. Run a cheap coarse pilot first.

**Another correction:** The current Polyakov implementation hard-codes cubic coordinate directions. For the diamond lattice, non-contractible cycles must be identified explicitly from the cell complex's homology generators.
