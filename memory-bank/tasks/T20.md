## Overview
Monte Carlo simulation of Z₂ lattice gauge theory across 2D and 3D lattices. Studying phase transitions, finite-size scaling, and critical behavior.

## Phases Completed

### ✅ Phase 1: 2D Square Lattice (T20a)
- L=8, 8 β values (0.20–1.00), 500k sweeps
- Confirmed smooth crossover (no phase transition in 2D)
- Rust validation benchmark (T27) matches TypeScript results

### ✅ Phase 2: 2D Multi-Lattice FSS (T20b)
- L=8,12,16,20,24 with 10 β values (0.30–0.60)
- Sharp peak in susceptibility confirms Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless behavior
- Critical β converges to ~0.45 for large L

### ✅ Phase 3: 3D Cubic Lattice (T20c)
- L=4,6,8 coarse scan (β=0.50–1.00, 10 values)
- L=8,16 fine scan (β=0.70–0.82, 25 values) 
- L=32 fine scan (β=0.74–0.78, 21 values) — 300k therm + 200k measure sweeps, 8 workers
- **First-order phase transition confirmed** — plaquette jumps from ~0.88 to ~0.96 at β≈0.758
- Binder cumulant stabilizes at U≈0.666 (3D Ising universal value)
- Peak susceptibility χ=1.3704 at β=0.758
- Peak specific heat C=1.0388 at β=0.758
