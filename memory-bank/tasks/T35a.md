## Scope

Distinguish the literal CZX on-site symmetry from the structural correspondence between the CZX code qubit and the LQG intertwiner qubit. Construct explicit many-body CZX states and verify their symmetry properties numerically.

## Completed

1. **Local operator audit (2026-07-18)** — `ts-quantum` four-qubit CZX audit confirms $U_{\mathrm{CZX}}^2 = I$ but leaks out of SU(2) intertwiner subspace. Expected negative result.
2. **Single plaquette construction (2026-07-18)** — $|\Psi\rangle = (|0000\rangle + |1111\rangle)/\sqrt2$ is exact +1 eigenvector of $U_{CZX}$. Verified numerically.
3. **Open plaquette boundary signature** — 3-edge open plaquette shows relative sign ($\langle\Psi|U|\Psi\rangle = 0$), confirming boundary obstruction.
4. **2×2 torus (16 qubits)** — Global $\prod_s U_{CZX,s}$ preserves state; single-site $U_{CZX,s}$ maps to orthogonal state. CZ cancellation on shared links is the mechanism.
5. **Parent Hamiltonian (2026-07-21)** — Verified H = Σ_p h_p is a valid parent Hamiltonian for the CZX state. Unique gapped ground state (gap = 1.0), commuting projector, frustration-free.

## Key Finding

The CZX symmetry is a **global dressed product**, not an on-site operator. The state is a trivial product of plaquette GHZ states, but the symmetry cannot be disentangled on-site — this is the hallmark of a nontrivial SPT.
