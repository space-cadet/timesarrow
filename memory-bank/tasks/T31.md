## Objective

Implement and measure a **signed volume observable** for the Z₂ lattice gauge theory simulations. The key insight from the paper is that the signed volume operator $\hat{Q}_v$ (not the positive-definite $\hat{V}_v = \sqrt{|\hat{Q}_v|}$) should be used for composite systems. The emergence of a global time orientation is then tied to the emergence of a macroscopic geometry with non-vanishing, extensive signed volume.

## Background

In standard LQG, the volume operator is always positive: $\hat{V} = \sqrt{|\hat{Q}|}$. This discards the orientation information. The paper's framework uses the signed operator $\hat{Q}$ to distinguish the two time-orientation sectors. For a composite system:

$$\hat{Q}_{\text{total}} = \sum_{v \in S} \hat{Q}_v$$

- **Confined phase** (random Z₂ orientations): Signs cancel. $\langle |\hat{Q}_{\text{total}}| \rangle \sim \sqrt{N}$ (random walk).
- **Deconfined phase** (ordered Z₂): Signs align. $\langle |\hat{Q}_{\text{total}}| \rangle \sim N$ (extensive).

This means the confinement-deconfinement transition is simultaneously:
1. The emergence of a global arrow of time
2. The emergence of a macroscopic geometry with non-vanishing, extensive signed volume

## Implementation

### Gauge Fixing

The signed volume at individual vertices is gauge-dependent (a local Z₂ flip at a vertex changes all incident vertex signs). To measure it, we fix a gauge by choosing a **maximal spanning tree** from the origin and setting all links on the tree to +1. In practice, for a cubic lattice, we use the path: x-links first, then y-links, then z-links.

For a vertex at $(x,y,z)$, the sign is:
$$\text{sign}(x,y,z) = \prod_{i=0}^{x-1} \sigma_{(i,0,0),x} \times \prod_{j=0}^{y-1} \sigma_{(x,j,0),y} \times \prod_{k=0}^{z-1} \sigma_{(x,y,k),z}$$

**Expected:** |Q|/N ~ 1/√N = 0.125. Results are consistent with random-walk scaling across all β, confirming no deconfined phase in 2D.
