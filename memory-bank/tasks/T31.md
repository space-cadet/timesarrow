## Overview

Use signed volume operator Q̂ (not positive-definite V̂) for composite systems, so that emergence of global time orientation is tied to emergence of macroscopic signed volume.

## Motivation

Standard LQG uses $\hat{V} = \sqrt{|\hat{Q}|}$. This discards sign information that distinguishes the two time-orientation sectors. The paper's framework requires using the **signed** volume operator $\hat{Q}$ for composite systems:

$$\hat{Q}_{\text{total}} = \sum_{v \in S} \hat{Q}_v$$

## Issue: Gauge-Dependence

The signed volume at individual vertices is **gauge-dependent**:
- Local Z₂ gauge transformation at vertex v flips σ_e → -σ_e on all incident edges
- This flips sign of Q̂_v but leaves V̂_v unchanged
- Absolute sign of Q̂_v is not physical

**Current gauge-fixing:** Single path from origin to each vertex. This may not be sufficient for reliable measurements.

**Fix needed:** Iterative gauge-fixing to ensure consistency.
