% Z2 Action and Time Reversal in Spin Networks
% Section 10: Formalization of the Z2 Gauge Field

The introduction of a local $Z_2$ symmetry on the spin network edges allows us to gauge the time-reversal operation. Following Chen and Vishwanath \cite{Chen2015Gauging}, we define a $Z_2$ gauge field $\sigma_{e} \in \{+1, -1\}$ living on the edges of the graph $\Gamma$. The action of this gauge field on the edge labels $j_e$ is to implement a local time-reversal transformation.

In the 1-st order formalism of LQG, the fundamental variables are the $SU(2)$ connection $A_a^i$ and the densitized triad $E^a_i$. Under a local $Z_2$ transformation at a vertex $v$, the triads on the edges connected to $v$ transform as:
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:z2-triad-transform}
    E^a_i \rightarrow \sigma_{v} E^a_i
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_v$ is the value of the $Z_2$ gauge field at the vertex. This transformation preserves the metric $h_{ab} = E^a_i E^b_i \delta^{ij}$ but, as noted in the Discussion, changes the sign of the determinant of the triad:
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:z2-det-triad}
    \det({}^3 e) \rightarrow \sigma_v^3 \det({}^3 e) = \sigma_v \det({}^3 e)
\end{equation}
Since the $Z_2$ field can vary from vertex to vertex, we have a theory with a local choice of the orientation of space, which is equivalent to a local choice of the direction of time evolution.

The spontaneous symmetry breaking of this $Z_2$ gauge field occurs when the system enters a phase where the expectation value of the order parameter $\langle \sigma_v \rangle \neq 0$. In our cosmological context, this transition occurs at the Planck scale. A non-zero $\langle \sigma_v \rangle$ throughout the bulk of the spin network implies a global alignment of the "local arrows of time," leading to the emergence of a macroscopic, cosmological arrow of time.

The order parameter $\sigma$ thus plays the role of a "time-orientation" field. Regions where $\sigma = +1$ correspond to our "future-pointing" arrow, while regions with $\sigma = -1$ would represent "anti-spacetime" where time flows in the opposite direction. The stability of our current Universe suggests we are in a broken-symmetry phase where $\sigma$ is uniform over observable scales, preventing the chaotic mixing of temporal orientations.
