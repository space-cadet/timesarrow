% Mapping the CZX state to LQG Intertwiners
% Section 8: SPT Phase of Quantum Geometry

The mapping between the CZX model and the spin network states of Loop Quantum Gravity is founded on the structural equivalence of their fundamental building blocks. As shown in \autoref{fig:czx-site}, each site in the CZX model consists of four spins. This is mathematically identical to the Hilbert space of a 4-valent spin network vertex where each edge carries a $j=1/2$ representation of $SU(2)$.

The ground state of the CZX model is characterized by the entanglement of neighboring spins across different sites, forming plaquettes as seen in \autoref{fig:czx-entangled}. In the LQG context, this corresponds to the generation of a specific intertwiner at each vertex. The CZX ground state $\ket{\Psi_{CZX}}$ can be expressed as a product of plaquette states:
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:czx-product-state}
    \ket{\Psi_{CZX}} = \prod_{p} \ket{\psi_p} = \prod_{p} \left( \ket{0000}_p + \ket{1111}_p \right)
\end{equation}
When we project this state onto the $SU(2)$ invariant subspace of the vertices, we select the sector that satisfies the Gauss constraint \eqref{eqn:gauss-operator-v2}. For a 4-valent vertex, the invariant subspace is two-dimensional, spanned by the states $\ket{\Psi_1}$ and $\ket{\Psi_2}$ defined in \eqref{eqn:vol-states}.

The key realization is that the CZX symmetry-protected topological order corresponds to a specific "superposition" of these quantum tetrahedral geometries. Specifically, the on-site $Z_2$ symmetry of the CZX model, generated by $U_{CZX}$, acts on the intertwiner degrees of freedom. In the SPT phase, the entanglement is short-range (SRE), ensuring that the emergent geometry is local and smooth, as argued in \autoref{sec:topo-order}.

Furthermore, the gapless edge excitations of the CZX model, which appear when a boundary is introduced, correspond to the emergence of Dirac fermions in the quantum gravity theory. These fermions are the physical manifestation of the breakdown of the SPT order at the "edge" of space or at defect sites, providing a geometric origin for matter degrees of freedom.
